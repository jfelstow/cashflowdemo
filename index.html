Path to Positive Cash Flow
The locked recommendation · Enterprise Win + Hire Now + $150K line of credit + cost discipline
The Locked Plan The 13-week window runs Net −$22,600 — fixed payroll against lumpy project revenue. The fix isn’t cutting to the bone; it’s growing into the fixed cost. Win the enterprise retainer, staff it now, bridge the gap with a $150K line of credit, and hold costs. With the bridge in place, cash never dips below your starting line — lowest — — and grows to — in 12 months. Without the bridge, hiring ahead of the client pulls you to ~$107K (and through the floor with no cost discipline). Works only if all three legs hold: the client signs, the hires perform, and spend stays tight.

☰ The Plan
12 steps
This month · Q3 · Q4 · Next year
This Month
  1. Close the enterprise retainer. Get the SOW signed at $35K/month, 12-month term, Net 30. Everything downstream depends on this signature. +$35,000/mo
  2. Open a $150K line of credit before you need it. Draw $150K in week 6 to bridge payroll until enterprise cash lands. ~$1,500/wk service
  3. Make the two senior hires (account director + senior designer). Start them now so they’re ramped before Q4 delivery. +$4,600/wk
  4. Tighten discretionary spend — pause non-essential software, scale back travel, defer the office buildout. +$2,500/wk
Q3 (Jul – Sep)
  1. Onboard and ramp the new hires on existing accounts first, so they’re productive before the enterprise work begins.
  2. Pull project receivables forward. Offer a small early-pay discount to smooth the late-July trough — the tightest point in the year.
  3. Stand up enterprise delivery — scope, staffing plan, and kickoff so Q4 billing starts on time.
The risk to watch Hiring ahead of revenue is the whole bet. If the enterprise client slips a quarter or churns early, you’re carrying $4,600/wk of new payroll plus line-of-credit service against the current book. The line of credit is the safety margin — don’t spend it on anything but this bridge.
Q4 (Oct – Dec)
  1. Enterprise billing begins. Revenue ramps to the full $35K/month run-rate over the quarter.
  2. First enterprise cash arrives (Net 30 on the first deliverables). This is the week the whole plan has been building toward.
  3. Begin repaying the line of credit once enterprise cash is reliably flowing — target a zero balance within the year.
Next Year
  1. Line of credit repaid, cash building. Revisit owner distributions and the deferred office buildout from a position of strength.
  2. Lock in a second enterprise account using the proven delivery model — now you have the team and the playbook.
